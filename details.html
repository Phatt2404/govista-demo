About Santorini

Santorini, officially known as Thira, is a volcanic island in the Cyclades group of the Greek islands. It is famous for its dramatic views, stunning sunsets, white-washed houses, and its very own active volcano.

The island was the site of one of the largest volcanic eruptions in recorded history: the Minoan eruption, which occurred about 3,600 years ago. The eruption left a large caldera surrounded by volcanic ash deposits hundreds of meters deep and may have contributed to the collapse of the Minoan civilization on the island of Crete.

What to Expect

Santorini is considered one of the most romantic and beautiful destinations in the world, known for:

  • Breathtaking caldera views and famous sunsets, especially from the village of Oia
  • Unique architecture with white-washed cubic buildings adorned with blue accents
  • Volcanic beaches with black, red, and white sand
  • Local wineries producing unique wines from the volcanic soil
  • Archaeological sites including Ancient Thera and Akrotiri

Best Time to Visit

The best times to visit Santorini are April-May and September-October when the weather is warm, and the crowds are smaller. The peak tourist season is during the summer months (June-August) when temperatures are highest but the island is most crowded.

Key Attractions

Oia Village

Famous for its stunning sunset views and picturesque blue-domed churches.

Fira

The vibrant capital of Santorini with shopping, dining, and panoramic views.

Red Beach

A unique beach with red volcanic cliffs and sand against the blue Aegean Sea.

Akrotiri Archaeological Site

An ancient Minoan city preserved under volcanic ash, often called the "Prehistoric Pompeii".
